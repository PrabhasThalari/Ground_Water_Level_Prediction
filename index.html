METHODOLOGY
How It Works
A 5-stage pipeline from raw hydrological data to accurate water level predictions.
STEP 01
📂

Data Loading

Upload groundwater level and rainfall CSV data spanning 10 years from the Udupi district observation well.

STEP 02
🔧

Preprocessing

Remove missing values, encode categorical labels, and apply Min-Max normalisation across all features.

STEP 03
🧬

Feature Selection

A binary Genetic Algorithm selects the most informative feature subset, reducing 11 inputs down to ~4.

STEP 04
🤖

GA Optimisation

Crow Search or Grey Wolf Optimizer tunes ANN weights — escaping local minima that trap standard backprop.

STEP 05
📊

Evaluation

The trained ANN predicts water levels on unseen test data. MSE and R² metrics evaluate model performance.

OPTIMISATION METHODS
Algorithm Comparison
Two bio-inspired optimisers compete to train the ANN with the lowest prediction error.
🐦 Crow Search Algorithm

Crow Search GA

Mimics crows' intelligent memory-based foraging behaviour. Each crow remembers the best position and follows others with a tunable awareness probability to avoid local traps.

Population of crows randomly initialised in weight space
Awareness probability (AP) prevents premature convergence
Flight length (FL) controls exploration vs exploitation
Typical MSE ≈ 0.25 — fast convergence, moderate accuracy
🐺 Grey Wolf Optimizer

Grey Wolf GA

Simulates the strict social hierarchy of grey wolves — alpha, beta, delta and omega — to collaboratively guide the search for globally optimal ANN weights.

Alpha, Beta, Delta wolves guide the pack's movement
Linear decay of parameter 'a' from 2 to 0 over iterations
Collaborative encircling enables strong local search
Typical MSE ≈ 0.08 — superior prediction accuracy ✓
